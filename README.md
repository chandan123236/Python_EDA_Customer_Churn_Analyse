# Python_EDA_Customer_Churn_Analyse

Summary and Recommendations
Objective

The analysis investigates key factors contributing to customer churn, with a primary focus on contract types, payment methods, service subscriptions, internet service types, and customer tenure. The goal is to identify high-risk segments and recommend actionable strategies to improve customer retention.

Customers on month-to-month contracts experience the highest churn rate (42%), significantly higher than those on one-year (11%) and two-year contracts (3%).

Insight: Long-term contracts substantially improve retention and strengthen customer commitment.

Internet Service Type

Fiber-optic users churn at 30%, higher than DSL users at 20%.

Insight: Dissatisfaction with pricing, competition, or service quality may contribute to churn; quality improvements or targeted support programs may help.

Senior Citizens

Senior citizens demonstrate a 41% churn rate, significantly higher than the 26% churn rate among non-senior customers.

Insight: Personalized support and specialized plans may significantly improve retention.

Bar charts and stacked percentage plots clearly reveal the contrast in churn distribution across payment methods, contract types, and tenure groups.

Line and comparative graphs show a strong decline in churn with increasing tenure, reinforcing the importance of early engagement.

Service-based visualizations highlight that customers without support services like Tech Support, Online Security, and Backup plans churn at disproportionately high rates.

üìù Recommendations

Promote Long-Term Contracts: Offer loyalty discounts, bundle pricing, or reward programs to shift customers from monthly plans to annual contracts.

Encourage Secure Payment Methods: Educate customers on safer, more stable payment options and consider incentive-based transitions away from electronic checks.

Strengthen Early-Stage Engagement: Leverage onboarding campaigns, personalized offers, and proactive support during the first year.

Target Senior Customers: Implement dedicated care programs, simplified service plans, and direct assistance channels to reduce churn.

Improve Service Experience for Fiber Users: Investigate service quality concerns and competitive benchmarking to design tailored improvement plans.

üìå Conclusion

The analysis clearly demonstrates that churn is heavily influenced by contract structure, payment behavior, service adoption, and tenure duration. Prioritizing customer experience during the early months, promoting long-term commitment, and reducing friction in payment and support services can substantially increase retention and lifetime customer value.
